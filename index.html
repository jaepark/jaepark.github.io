Elizabeth Yuk

Elizabeth began her cello studies at age 12 and became actively involved with cello performance by joining the Seattle Youth Symphony Orchestras and later entering in music competitions. She has received numerous prize awards, including First Place in the WA State Youth Music Competition and had the opportunity to solo with the Eastside Symphony Orchestra.

Elizabeth graduated from the University of Washington with a Bachelor of Music in Cello Performance and studied under the tutelage of Toby Saks, Eric Gaenslen, and Sæunn Thorsteinsdóttir. During her studies at the UW, Elizabeth was the recipient of the Delores Gail Plath Music Scholarship. She also served as principal cellist of the UW Symphony Orchestra. Elizabeth is also actively involved in chamber music and has performed in solo master classes with cellists Hans Jorgen Jensen and Ani Kalayjian.

Elizabeth believes music is deeply personal and provides avenues for self-expression. As a teacher, she focuses on building solid technical foundations in her students to help them play challenging pieces as well as encourage creative thinking and performance.
